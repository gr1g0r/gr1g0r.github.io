Video Lecture 8 "How to Get Started, Doing Things that Don't Scale, Press"

Oct 1, 2017  

Hello everyone! Today’s video lecture’s speaker is Stanley Tang, who is the founder of DoorDash.

Well, at first Stanley was telling about how everything started in DoorDash and how they tested whether delivery system was a good idea. What they did, is pretty simple! They just launched a demo website in an hour and tested if they would get orders.

The main message Stanley tried to give was that you don’t need much to start a startup. You need to launch it as fast as you can, and at first you shouldn’t scale. Scale only where there is the demand for it.

The next speaker is Walker Williams who is the founder and CEO of Teespring. He is speaking about “Three places to leverage your advantage.”

Finding your first users

According to Walker there’s no silver bullet for user acquisition! The majorities of companies gained their first users very very hard. The founder of company has the responsibility to do whatever it takes to get the first users. After that, when users add up, everything will get a bit easier.

Turning users into champions

According to Walker a champion is the user who talks about your product or recommends it to others. The best way to turn your users into champions is to talk to them. But how we need to talk with them? Well, Walker recommends these three ways to talk with your customers.

  1. Run customer service as long as possible
  2. Proactive reach out to current and churned customers
  3. Social media

Finding product/market fit

An important point here is that the product you launch at first, will not end up being the product that you will scale up with. In a startup you may have dirty and not right code as the most important thing is that it has to work, and it has to work as soon as possible.

The 3rd speaker is Justin Kan, who appears to be the founder of Justin.tv, and, the more famous one Twitch. In this video he is speakig about PR.

Well, to be honest this part of the video was the most interesting for me. I always wanted to know how the new and not famous products appear in websites like theVerge or HackerNews.

As it appears, it doesn’t happen magically. At first you need to have a goal, which you will achieve by appearing in media. For example in case of Twitch, the goal was to reach to the customers in Gaming Industry.

But for appearing in press, you need to have a story. Stories can be different, it is important to have a story which will help you achieve the goal you set up earlier. It doesn’t need to be original, it need to be interesting.
