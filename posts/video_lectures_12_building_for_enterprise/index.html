Video Lecture 12 "Building for the enterprise"

Oct 24, 2017  

Hello everyone, Today I am going to review another video about how to start a startup. Today the guest lecturer is Aaron Levie who is the CEO and co-founder of the Box, and he is going to speak about building enterprise software.

Box is a cloud content management and file sharing service for businesses. In the lecture Aaron talks about the history of the Box. According to him, at first they didn’t plan to create enterprise software, they just noticed that file sharing is was a really hard procedure and tried to fix that by creating the Box.

According to Aaron, while building the Box, it came time when their product had far more features than an average user needs, and not enough features for enterprise companies. And that is when they had to decide which path to go. After much thinking they decided to give it a shot and try to sell their product to enterprise, even when everyone around told them that they will fail.

Aaron thinks that now is the perfect time for building for enterprise, since now the technology is moving to cloud, it is easier to deliver and manage the software. Another important factor is that today computing becomes cheaper.

As soon as the computing becomes cheaper, its easier to adopt new solutions. Which means, their barrier for showing you in the barrier is a lot lower which is great for startups

Today the world is changing rapidly, hence the enterprises try to adapt to the new need of the customers. For that they are going through a lot of changes too and that is the best time for startups to come in and suggest their solutions to the enterprises.

In enterprise it is better to start small and create a product that will slip in the gaps of other existing products but will expand to a more important product.

Listen to your customers but don’t always build exactly what they are telling you

What this means is that you need to listen to all of their requests and problems, and at the end give the simplified solution.
