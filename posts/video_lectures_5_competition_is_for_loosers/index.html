Video Lecture 5 "Competition is for losers"

Sep 16, 2017  

Hey Everyone! In this post I am going to write about my takeaways from this lecture of Peter Thiel, who, if you don’t know, is the founder of PayPal and is going to talk about how to start a startup.

So basically what peter concentrates on is that for a successful startup you need a market where you will have monopoly.

For me, the interesting part in this video starts from the part about how to create a monopoly. Peter’s advice is to go for a small market, where there is small or almost no competition.

What is also important is to start something that either doesn’t exist, or you can do several times better. If you’re creating something create in way, so you will be the last company doing it in that field. For example Facebook is the last to create social media, Google is the last one who created search engine, etc.

We often think about losers as people who are bad at competing or don’t want to compete. Although competition is a very good thing, as it makes you realize what you are good at, motivates you to work on yourself and be better, it makes you to stop ask bigger and more important questions. So do something that no one did before, and your chances to succeed will be much bigger.
