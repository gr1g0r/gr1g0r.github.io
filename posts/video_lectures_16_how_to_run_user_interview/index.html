Video Lecture 16 "How to Run a User Interview"

Dec 17, 2017  

Hello everybody, today I am going to review another video lectre about starting a successful startup, and today’s guest lecturer is Emmett Shear. Emmett is the CEO of Twitch, which is the most popular platform among gamers (and not only) for game streaming. In this lecture Emmett is going to speak about “How to Run a User Interview”.

At first Emmett starts by telling about his first startups. The first startup was the “Kiko Calendar”. Which was good, but didn’t go too far. Since they didn’t know much about calendars, they used to ask about calendars to anybody who used them. Which was not optimal and they never got to the talk to users part.

The second startup was the Justin.tv: A website for people to broadcast their lives. Since the primary users of the website were themselves, they didn’t do the talk to users part this time as well. The only problem was that they didn’t know how to develop the product further. The idea that Emmett came up with was in game industry: a platform where people could broadcast video games. And that is how the Twitch was born.

According to Emmett, you don’t need to ask the user questions about optimizing user flow in the first interview, since that will distract the users, who mostly think that they know what do they want.

Emmett states, that during the first period of Justin.tv gaming platform, the users of the platform addressed several issues. While many people would go and try to solve them, that’s not what Emmett and his team did. According to him, if the problems users tell about really exist and they still use them, than maybe that problems aren’t big enough. It is better to address problems and features hat will bring people who are not currently your customers.

Another good idea by Emmett is that you shouldn’t concentrate only on the current market and try to attract the customers of your competitors. What you really need to do is to try to bring people who didn’t even try to use that kind of products.

These were the most interesting ideas for me found in the video. If you have other interesting ideas, don’t hesitate to write a comment.
