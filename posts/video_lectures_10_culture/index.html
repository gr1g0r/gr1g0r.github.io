Video Lecture 10 "Culture"

Oct 22, 2017  

Hello everybody, after some delay I am going to review another video from Stanford University lectures. This time the gust speakers are Brian Chesky and Alfred Lin.

The first speaker is Alfred Lin.

According to Lin the company culture is

Everyday core values and actions of each member of the team in pursuit of our mission.

Honesty, integrity, service and teamwork are some core values of startup culture. Each of them is an important factor that together can create a successful team.

Lin tells, that Patrick Lencioni in his book “The Five Dydfunctions of a Team “ wrote about Elements of high performing teams. That elements are

  • Results
  • Accountability
  • Commitment
  • Conflict
  • Trust

So what are the best practices for startup culture? Well, According to Lin, to develop the startup culture people need to

  • Incorporate their mission to their values
  • Performance = think harder, deeper, longer about values
  • Interview for culture fit
  • Evaluate performance on culture as well
  • Make daily daily habit

The next speaker is Brian Chesky, who is the CEO of Airbnb. Instead of having a talk, he had a Q&A, and I am going to write some of the ideas I liked.

  1. While everyone is concentrated on creating a product, building the company is also equally important, as without a good company, even the best products may fail.
  2. Airbnb hires people who want missions.
  3. If you don’t give a crap then you shouldn’t be here.
  4. Having a clear mission making sure that mission comes through the company is the most important you can do for your company’s values.
  5. If you get 100 people who love your company, they are going to tell others and attract more and more customers. In fact it is better to have 100 users who love you, instead of millions of users who just use your app.

These were just my takeaways from the video, I recommend it to watch as there are many things which I skipped in my post but you may find interesting. Do you have your opinions about this topic? Let’s discuss them in comments.
