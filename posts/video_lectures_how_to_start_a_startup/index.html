Video lecture 1 review "How to start a startup"

Sep 16, 2017  

This video’s first speaker is the president of Y Combinator Sam Altman. One of the biggest investment in IT are that Y Combinator had is its Hacker News website. As a daily user of the Hacker News it became more interesting for me what this guy will talk about.

Well, I have to say that the talk was quite interesting and informative. He mainly talked about the four areas that you have to excel at to be able to start a successful startup.

  1. Idea
  2. Product
  3. Team
  4. Execution

So let’s start with the first one

The Idea

According to Sam, the idea is one of the most important, as even if the greatest executioners work on a terrible idea, most probably it will fail. So you have to have an idea, that you believe is good enough and matters enough to work on it in upcoming 10 years or more. If the idea will become not actual after a short time, then it doesn’t worth your time.

One of the main aspects you need to concentrate on while choosing the idea is the market. You have to choose a market that grows rapidly. Small but rapidly growing market is far better than the big and slowly growing one.

So choose your idea carefully, develop it around some mission that matters for you and the team and don’t forget about the market.

The Product

Often you can have a great idea but fail to crate a good product. To form a successful company you need to be able to turn the great idea into a great product. For that you have to keep customers in mind and create something that they will love to use.

In fact, it is better to create something that small number of users will love, or something that a huge number of users will like. Remember that it is easier to expand over a great product.

An important part of creating a great product is paying attention to the feedback. It is important to ask users for feedback and make decision based on that feedback. After all, the users are the ones who will use your product or pay for it.

But Why to start a startup?

Well, about the other the team and the execution Sam will speak during another lecture. Until that, Dustin Moskovitzi, one of the co-founders of Facebook, talks about why you should start a startup.

At first Dustin points out that starting a start is not as glamorous as it may seem at the first glance. Starting a startup has an ugly side as well, which is sitting in front of same desk for hours, trying to solve a difficult problem or answer to customers.

Also, if you think that you will feel like boss in your company, according to Dustin this is not the case. More probably everyone else will feel as boss. You will not have any free time, will have to work 24⁄7, always be on call.

If you have a great idea and an equally good product, most probably you will have good financial reward and thus have more impact on the world around you.

So, if you have a great idea, a passion towards that idea and feel like you are the one that has to do it, then you just can’t not do it. In that case the best thing is going for it and doing your best to succeed.

Some discussion

In my opinion, you have to start a startup, if you believe that somehow it will change the world. I disagree with Sam that if there isn’t market for the product yet, creating one is a bad idea. There are many cases in history, that people didn’t know that the needed something, and after the inventors brought the product to the market, people realised how much they needed it. In my opinion, if you believe that you can change the world, the remaining doesn’t matter, just go for it.
