Video Lecture 19 "Sales and Marketing; How to Talk to Investors"

Dec 17, 2017  

Good evening everybody! I am going to review the 19th lecture about starting a startup. This video lecture’s speaker is Tyler Bosmeny, who is the founder of Clever.

According to Tyler sales isn’t that difficult for the founder. In fact all he has to is to talk to users. That’s it! Being the founder gives you advantage that you have the passion towards what you are building and you know the industry and the problem you are solving with your product.

What I’ve learned is that when it comes to “hiring the sales people,” as a founder, the reality is that it’s you

According to Tyler there are three ways to attract people to use your product. The first one is your personal network. The second one is conferences, and the last one is cold mails.

There is a common mistake that according to Tyler most founders do. That is when they talk to companies, the company says for example “I like your product, but it lacks this feature which I need”. Most founders try to add that feature quickly, thinking that that way the company will actually buy the product, but in most of cases that is not working that way.

So what you should do in that cases? According to Tyler you can suggest to sign the agreement, after which you will implement the needed feature, or you can say “Okay, let’s wait and see if other customers have need that feature or not. If they do, I will add the feature and reach back to you”.

Another advice from Tyler is to avoid free-trials. In startup you need revenue, validation, users and commitment. None of those things you can get by offering free trials. If you are requested to give a free trial of your product what you can do is simply say

We don’t do free trials. We do annual agreements and what we’ll do is for the first 30 or 60 days, if for any reason you’re not happy, you can opt out.

These were my takeouts from this lecture. If you have other ideas or lied other points in the lecture let’s discuss them in the comment section.
