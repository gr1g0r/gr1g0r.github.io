Video Lecture 3 "Before the Startup"

Sep 16, 2017  

Hey everyone! In this post I am going to review another lecture about starting startups. The speaker in this lecture is Paul Graham, Co-Founder of Y combinator and my favorite news blog Hacker News.

Paul once more highlights the importance of choosing the right team members. According to him we must choose team members as we would choose our friends.

Another part I find important in the video is that you don’t need to be an expert in startups, you just need to work with your users in the right way. That means there is still hope for us :).

Another advice I find useful is that before trying to find investors, startups need to start building the product, provide growth and by doing so they will automatically attract investors.

For learning how to start a startup, the most effective way is to actually start one, no one can actually teach you how to communicate with users or how to handle difficult situations.

Starting a startup can be really hard. If you are terrified from the difficulties, it’s better not to start. If you are not sure, the risk it. Unless you risk, no one can tell whether you will be able to handle all the difficulties and create a successful startup, or whether you will fail. :w
