Video Lecture 11 "Hiring and culture"

Oct 22, 2017  

Hello everyone, today’s video lecture’s guests are Patrick and John Collison and Ben Slibermann. This was more a Q&A than a usual lecture and this time, instead of reviewing the video I am going to write out the ideas that I liked.

  • The decisions while you hire your first 10 employees are one of the mot important ones, as by hiring those 10 people, you are actually hiring 100 people.
  • Culture is more like gardening than an architecture.
  • Many of your first employees will be new to the field, as more experienced people would rather to stay an d work in big companies, than join a startup which just started.
  • Make teams feel as autonomous and nimble as possible.
  • Either your company fails really quickly or all of your problems become about management growth.
  • In one year, you are thinking a year ahead. After four years you are thinking four years ahead.
  • One of the benefits of working at a startup is you can be handed a challenge no one else would be crazy enough to let you take on.
  • If you tell people, “Hey we are going to go to Mars”, it attracts the best people and you are closer to Mars.

These were the most interesting ideas for me found in the video. If you have other interesting ideas, don’t hesitate to write a comment.
